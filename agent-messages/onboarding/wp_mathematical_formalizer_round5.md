## 9. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### 9.1 Mathematical Pitfalls
- **Informal definitions** - All definitions must be mathematically precise
- **Missing hypotheses** - Theorems must state all conditions
- **Incomplete proofs** - Proofs must be complete, not sketches
- **Notation drift** - Must follow notation guide strictly

### 9.2 Process Pitfalls
- **Working in isolation** - Must collaborate with Technical Writers
- **Last-minute formalization** - Mathematics should be integrated early
- **No version control** - Definitions evolve, need tracking
- **Ignoring feedback** - Mathematics must be understandable to readers

### 9.3 Collaboration Pitfalls
- **Assuming mathematical knowledge** - Explain concepts clearly
- **Not aligning with writers** - Mathematics should support narrative
- **Over-formalizing** - Balance rigor with readability
- **Under-communicating** - Regular updates to team
